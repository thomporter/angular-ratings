Angular Ratings Cool Angular Rating Icons Included!

A diretive to help out with rating systems on your Angular powered website.

In the first 2 demos, we're binding directly to a property on the scope of our controller, probably not ever going to happen in the real world, but it makes for a super simple exmpale.

Demo 1 Interactive

A ratings widget that is editable by the user.

<angular-ratings ng-model="user_rating" notify-url="notify.json" notify-id="id">

Demo 2 Display

Based on a static value not editable by the user, good for showing the average rating of something. This is accomplished by simply leaving out the notify-url. 

<angular-ratings ng-model="user_rating">

Demo 3 Usage in a list

In this demo we'll be a little more realistic. Here our controller has a list of items, each with an id, name and rating. It also has an object called "user_ratings" that represents the active user's ratings. It uses the item id's as keys, with the user's rating as values. This allows us to access the ratings for each item like so: user_ratings[item.id]

{{item.name}}
Your Rating
Average Rating 
<div ng-repeat="item in items" class="item">
  <h5>{{item.name}}</h5>
  <div class="row-fluid">
     <div class="span6">
        <strong>Your Rating</strong>
        <angular-ratings notify-url="notify.json" notify-id="id" ng-model="user_ratings[item.id]"></angular-ratings>
     </div>
     <div class="span6">
        <strong>Average Rating</strong>
        <angular-ratings ng-model="item.avg_rating"></angular-ratings>
     </div>
  </div>
</div>
